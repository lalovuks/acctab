Bookkeeping for Small Businesses in Botswana

The daily and monthly routines, reconciliations and cloud tools that keep a Botswana SME's records clean, BURS-ready and genuinely useful for running the business.

Updated July 2026 By the ACCTAB Professional Team 12 min read

Every financial statement, tax return and loan application your business will ever produce starts in the same place: the daily record of what came in and what went out. That is bookkeeping. Get it right and everything downstream — tax, financing, decisions — becomes easy. Get it wrong and even the best accountant is building on sand.

This guide gives Botswana small business owners a practical bookkeeping system: what to record daily, what to close monthly, how reconciliations work, and how cloud tools have made professional-grade bookkeeping affordable for even the smallest operation.

Bookkeeping vs Accounting: What's the Difference?

Bookkeeping records; accounting interprets. The bookkeeper captures every sale, purchase, payment and receipt as it happens. The accountant takes those records and produces financial statements, tax computations and advice. One cannot exist without the other — which is why ACCTAB offers them as a single, seamless service. The Daily Bookkeeping Routine

Fifteen minutes a day prevents weeks of year-end pain:

  • Record every sale — issue an invoice or till record for each transaction, including cash sales.
  • Capture every expense — photograph receipts immediately; a glovebox full of faded fuel slips is money lost.
  • Do a daily cash-up — count the till, reconcile to recorded sales, bank the cash. Differences investigated same day.
  • Note the unusual — stock taken for personal use, goods returned, money lent — one line now saves an hour of head-scratching later.

The Monthly Bookkeeping Routine

  1. Reconcile the bank (see below) — the anchor of the whole month.
  2. Review debtors — who owes you, for how long; send statements and chase the overdue.
  3. Review creditors — what you owe and when it is due, so payments are planned, not panicked.
  4. Count or sample stock — compare against purchases and sales to spot shrinkage.
  5. Process payroll and PAYE — see our payroll guide.
  6. Close the month — lock the period and produce a simple profit report. Now you are managing with facts.

Bank Reconciliations: The Control That Catches Everything

A bank reconciliation matches every line in your books against your bank statement and explains any difference. It sounds mundane; it is the single most powerful control a small business has. Reconciliations catch:

  • Sales money that never reached the bank
  • Duplicated or fraudulent payments and debit orders
  • Bank charges and errors you can dispute
  • Transactions recorded twice — or not at all

If your books have never been reconciled to the bank, treat your profit figures as unverified. The first reconciliation ACCTAB performs for a new client almost always surfaces surprises — sometimes in the client's favour.

Expense Tracking That Survives a BURS Review

An expense without a document is an expense BURS can disallow. The rule is simple: no receipt, no record, no deduction. Practical system:

  • Photograph every receipt the day you receive it (cloud apps file them automatically).
  • Pay by card or transfer wherever possible — the bank statement becomes your second witness.
  • Keep personal spending out entirely; use recorded drawings instead.
  • File supplier invoices by month, digitally.

Cloud Bookkeeping: The SME Game-Changer

Cloud platforms such as QuickBooks, Xero and Sage have changed what small businesses can afford. Bank feeds import transactions automatically, phone cameras capture receipts, invoices go out by email with payment links, and your accountant in Francistown sees the same live figures you see in Gaborone or Maun. Backups are automatic and off-site — no more books lost with a stolen laptop.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between bookkeeping and accounting?

Bookkeeping records daily transactions; accounting interprets them — financial statements, tax and advice. Bookkeeping is the foundation; accounting is the structure built on it.

Can I do my own bookkeeping?

For a very small, simple business, yes — especially with cloud software. As volume, employees and VAT grow, most owners find outsourcing costs less than DIY time plus mistakes.

How often should bookkeeping be done?

Capture weekly at minimum; close monthly with a bank reconciliation. Books updated once a year are archaeology, not bookkeeping.

What is a bank reconciliation?

Matching every book entry against the bank statement and explaining differences. It catches missing income, duplicate payments and fraud — the most important monthly control.

Which software works best for Botswana SMEs?

QuickBooks, Xero and Sage are all widely used. The right choice depends on your business — ACCTAB helps select, set up and run the right system.

What records must I keep for BURS?

Sales and purchase invoices, receipts, bank statements, cash records, payroll and VAT records, asset details — kept for several years. Digital copies are acceptable and safer.

How much does outsourced bookkeeping cost?

It scales with volume — typically a modest fixed monthly fee, far less than employing a bookkeeper. ACCTAB quotes after a free assessment.

My books are months behind. Where do I start?

With bank statements — they anchor everything. ACCTAB's catch-up service reconstructs records, brings filings current, then keeps you current monthly.

Do cash businesses need bookkeeping too?

Especially so — cash leaves no automatic trail. A daily cash-up sheet reconciled to banking transforms control over theft and leakage.

What is cloud bookkeeping?

Online software with automatic bank feeds, phone-camera receipt capture and live figures shared with your accountant from anywhere in Botswana, with automatic backups.

Will good bookkeeping reduce my accounting fees?

Dramatically — clean books make year-end statements and tax returns fast. Rushed year-end reconstruction is the most expensive accounting work there is.

Can ACCTAB train my staff to do the basics?

Yes — a common setup: your staff capture daily, ACCTAB reviews, reconciles and reports monthly. You keep control; we ensure quality.
