Company Secretarial Services Explained for Botswana Businesses

Annual returns, board resolutions, statutory registers and CIPA compliance — the invisible work that keeps your company legally alive, explained.

Updated July 2026 By the ACCTAB Professional Team 11 min read

Every registered company in Botswana lives a second, quieter life alongside its trading one — a legal life of registers, resolutions and filings with the Companies and Intellectual Property Authority (CIPA). Neglect that life and the consequences arrive suddenly: a blocked bank transaction because a resolution is missing, a penalty for a late annual return, or — in the worst case — a company struck off the register while still trading.

This guide explains what company secretarial work actually involves, what the Companies Act expects of directors, and why so many Botswana companies hand the whole function to ACCTAB.

What Company Secretaries Actually Do

The company secretarial function is the company's legal housekeeping:

  • Filing annual returns and keeping company details current with CIPA
  • Maintaining statutory registers — directors, shareholders, share transfers, beneficial owners
  • Preparing board and shareholder resolutions for significant decisions
  • Recording meeting minutes and keeping the company's records inspectable
  • Lodging changes — new directors, transferred shares, amended addresses or names
  • Advising directors on their Companies Act duties

Annual Returns: The Deadline That Kills Companies

Every company must file an annual return with CIPA confirming its details — directors, shareholders, addresses. Miss it and penalties accrue; keep missing it and the company can be struck off the register, ceasing to exist legally while its bank accounts, contracts and assets hang in limbo. Restoration is possible, but slow and expensive.

Board Resolutions: Paperwork That Unblocks Money

Directors' decisions of consequence — opening bank accounts, borrowing, appointing signatories, selling major assets, transferring shares — need formal resolutions. In practice, this is where companies feel secretarial neglect first: the bank will not process the facility because the resolution is missing, and the deal waits while it is drafted, signed and dated correctly. A maintained resolution file means transactions never stall on paperwork.

Shareholders and Statutory Registers

Who owns the company, who sits on its board, and who ultimately controls it must be recorded in current statutory registers — including beneficial ownership information, an area of growing regulatory focus. Informal share deals ("we agreed he owns 30%") with nothing filed are among the most common sources of bitter shareholder disputes in Botswana. If it isn't in the register and filed with CIPA, expect trouble proving it later.

Corporate Governance

Governance sounds like big-company language, but its core is simple and applies to a two-person company: decisions made by the right people, recorded properly, with directors understanding their legal duties — acting in the company's interest, avoiding conflicts, not trading recklessly. Good governance protects directors personally: when things go wrong, the recorded, properly-made decision is the director's defence.

ACCTAB Expert Tip

Before any significant transaction — new loan, new shareholder, big asset sale — ask one question: "Is there a resolution for this, and is the register current?" Five minutes of paperwork before beats five months of dispute after.

Business Changes That Must Be Filed

  • Appointment or resignation of directors
  • Share transfers and new share issues
  • Change of registered address or company name
  • Amendments to the constitution
  • Changes in beneficial ownership

Each has a proper process and a filing timeframe. Done late or informally, they surface later as blocked transactions and disputed ownership.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does a company secretary do in Botswana?

Files annual returns with CIPA, maintains statutory registers, prepares board and shareholder resolutions, records meetings and lodges company changes — the company's legal housekeeping.

Is a company secretary compulsory for every company?

Requirements vary by company type, but the secretarial duties themselves — registers, resolutions, filings — apply to every company. Outsourcing ensures they actually happen.

What is CIPA?

The Companies and Intellectual Property Authority — Botswana's registrar of companies, accessed through its online registration system for incorporations, returns and changes.

What happens if my company misses its annual return?

Penalties accrue, and persistent failure can lead to strike-off — the company ceases to exist legally, with accounts and contracts thrown into confusion. Restoration is costly and slow.

What are board resolutions and when do I need them?

Formal written directors' decisions — needed for bank accounts, loans, signatories, share transfers and major asset sales. Banks routinely demand them; missing ones stall transactions.

What statutory registers must be kept?

Directors, shareholders, share transfers and beneficial ownership, plus minutes and resolutions — all current and inspectable.

How do I change directors or shareholders?

Proper resolutions, updated registers and CIPA filings within required timeframes. Informal handshake changes are a leading source of shareholder disputes.

What is beneficial ownership disclosure?

Recording the natural persons who ultimately own or control the company. Incomplete information can block filings and banking relationships.

Why outsource company secretarial work?

It is deadline-driven, technical and invisible until it fails — and the failures (strike-off, penalties, disputes) cost far more than the service.
