12 Payroll Mistakes That Cost Botswana Businesses Money

Payroll errors don't announce themselves — they accumulate quietly in penalties, back-pay claims and lost trust. Here are the twelve we correct most often, and what each one costs.

Updated July 2026 By the ACCTAB Professional Team 11 min read

Payroll touches three unforgiving parties at once: BURS, the labour authorities, and your own employees. A mistake with any of them is expensive; a mistake with all three at once can sink a small business. Below are the twelve payroll failures we encounter most often across Botswana — each with the cost it creates and the fix that prevents it. For the full picture of how payroll should run, start with our Complete Payroll Guide for Employers in Botswana.

The 12 Mistakes

  1. Paying salaries late
  2. Wrong deductions
  3. PAYE errors
  4. Poor payroll records
  5. Missing leave records
  6. Incorrect overtime
  7. Payroll fraud & ghost employees
  8. Manual spreadsheets
  9. Compliance failures
  10. No backups
  11. No payroll audits
  12. No expert review

1. Paying salaries late

Late pay breaches the Employment Act and the contract, invites labour disputes, and — the hidden cost — makes your best people start job-hunting. A business that cannot pay on time signals it may not pay at all. Fix: a payroll calendar with a fixed cut-off and pay date, and a cash flow forecast that treats salaries as untouchable. ACCTAB's payroll runs release payments on schedule, every month.

2. Wrong deductions

Deducting too much (unauthorised deductions are unlawful) or too little (advances never recovered, pension contributions missed) both end in disputes and losses. Fix: every deduction traceable to a law, a contract or a signed authorisation — and each run reviewed against the previous month.

3. PAYE errors

The most expensive item on this list. Outdated tax tables, mishandled benefits and casual "net pay agreements" all under-deduct PAYE — and the shortfall, penalties and interest land on the employer, not the employee. Fix: compute PAYE on current BURS tables with taxable benefits handled correctly. This is core to ACCTAB's payroll service; see also our Botswana Business Tax Guide.

4. Poor payroll records

No contracts, no payslip copies, no remittance proofs. When BURS queries a figure or a former employee claims underpayment, you have nothing to answer with — and the party without documents usually loses. Fix: a complete payroll file per employee, retained for several years. Outsourced payroll produces this automatically.

5. Missing leave records

Untracked leave means employees accumulate (or claim) balances you cannot verify. On termination, unverifiable leave becomes a cash payout — often for days already taken. Fix: a leave register updated with every absence, reconciled to payroll. It is one of the cheapest liabilities to prevent.

6. Incorrect overtime

Paying overtime at flat rates instead of Employment Act rates (generally 1.5×, double on rest days and holidays) silently builds a back-pay liability that a single labour complaint can crystallise — for the whole workforce, going back years. Fix: overtime captured per employee per period and costed at the correct rates.

7. Payroll fraud and ghost employees

When one unsupervised person controls the whole payroll, ghost employees, inflated hours and diverted payments can run for years. We have seen payrolls where more than one "employee" existed only in the bank file. Fix: segregation of duties — preparation, approval and payment split between different people. Outsourcing to ACCTAB creates that separation instantly, even in a three-person business.

8. Manual spreadsheets

One dragged formula, one hidden row, and the whole month is wrong with no audit trail to show where. Spreadsheets also depend entirely on the one person who built them. Fix: professional payroll software — or a provider who runs it for you.

9. Compliance failures

Missed PAYE remittances, skipped annual reconciliations, unregistered employers: each failure attracts penalties and marks your business for closer BURS attention. Fix: a statutory calendar with every deadline — or an outsourced provider for whom your deadlines are their core job.

10. No payroll backups

Payroll history on one laptop is one theft, fire or hardware failure away from disaster — with BURS still expecting the records. Fix: cloud-based payroll with automatic, off-site backups.

11. No payroll audits

Payroll that is never independently checked drifts: errors become precedents, precedents become "policy". An annual review catches drift while it is still cheap to correct. Fix: a periodic independent payroll review — ACCTAB offers this as a standalone service, no takeover required.

12. No expert review of unusual events

Terminations, retrenchments, severance calculations and final-pay computations are where the biggest single-event losses happen. One mishandled termination can cost more than a decade of professional fees. Fix: before any unusual payroll event, get professional advice. Frequently Asked Questions

What is the most expensive payroll mistake?

PAYE errors. Under-deducted tax remains the employer's liability, plus BURS penalties and interest — and small monthly errors compound into large assessments.

Can employees claim against me for late salaries?

Yes — late pay breaches the Employment Act and contract, and can lead to labour disputes and orders against the employer, quite apart from the damage to morale.

How do I detect ghost employees?

Reconcile the payroll register to contracts and physical headcount, verify bank account ownership, and separate preparation from approval. An external payroll review surfaces ghosts quickly.

Is spreadsheet payroll risky?

Yes — no audit trail, silent formula breakage and manual tax tables. Professional software or an outsourced provider removes that whole class of error.

What records matter in a labour dispute?

Contracts, payslips, attendance/overtime records and leave registers. Employers lose winnable disputes simply because documents are missing.

How often should payroll be audited?

An independent review annually, with monthly controls (segregation of duties, run approval) in between. ACCTAB offers standalone payroll reviews.

What if I miss the PAYE deadline?

BURS levies penalties and interest, and repeated lateness attracts closer attention. Outsourced payroll makes the deadline automatic.

Must overtime be paid at a higher rate?

Generally yes — 1.5× normal rate, double on rest days and public holidays, for employees within the Employment Act's scope. Flat-rate overtime builds a back-pay liability.

Can ACCTAB take over payroll mid-year?

Yes — we reconcile year-to-date figures on takeover so the annual BURS reconciliation still balances.

How much does a payroll mistake typically cost?

Count BURS penalties, back-pay awards, dispute costs and staff turnover. One mishandled termination can exceed years of professional payroll fees.

What is segregation of duties?

The person preparing payroll should not approve and pay it. This single control is the strongest defence against fraud and error — and outsourcing achieves it instantly.
