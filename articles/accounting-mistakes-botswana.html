15 Accounting Mistakes Botswana Businesses Make (And How to Avoid Them)

Every mistake below has cost a real Botswana business real money — in BURS penalties, declined loans, hidden losses or outright fraud. Here is how to spot and fix each one.

Updated July 2026 By the ACCTAB Professional Team 12 min read

After working with SMEs across Botswana — retailers, transporters, construction firms, salons, NGOs and startups — the same accounting mistakes appear again and again. None of them come from bad intentions; they come from busy owners doing accounting last, at night, after the real work is done.

Each mistake below follows the same structure: the problem, a typical example, the risk it creates, and the practical solution — including where ACCTAB's services take the burden off your shoulders entirely.

The 15 Mistakes

  1. Mixing personal and business money
  2. No separate business bank account
  3. Missing receipts and invoices
  4. Never reconciling the bank
  5. Incorrect VAT treatment
  6. Ignoring debtors
  7. No stock records
  8. Undocumented owner loans
  9. Cash sales off the books
  10. Confusing profit with cash
  11. Only doing accounts at year end
  12. DIY tax returns
  13. No asset register
  14. Unqualified help
  15. No backups

1. Mixing personal and business money

Problem: Groceries, school fees and airtime paid from the business account; business stock paid from a personal card.

Example: A Francistown retailer "borrows" P2,000 from the till each month. At year end, the books show a loss — but the business actually made money that simply left informally.

Risk: Distorted profit figures, incorrect tax computations, and no way to prove business performance to a bank.

Solution: Pay yourself a fixed, recorded amount each month. ACCTAB sets up a clean drawings/salary structure so your figures stay honest — and tax-efficient.

2. Operating without a business bank account

Problem: All money flows through one personal account.

Risk: Bookkeeping becomes guesswork, BURS reviews become painful, and banks cannot assess the business for finance.

Solution: Open a dedicated business account and run everything through it. It is the cheapest accounting control that exists.

3. Missing receipts and invoices

Problem: Fuel, repairs and supplier payments with no documents.

Risk: BURS can disallow undocumented expenses — you pay income tax on money you already spent. Input VAT claims also fail without valid tax invoices.

Solution: Photograph every receipt the day you get it. ACCTAB's cloud bookkeeping setup lets you capture receipts from your phone in seconds.

4. Never reconciling the bank

Problem: The books have never been matched line-by-line against the bank statement.

Example: A transport company only discovered ten months of duplicated debit-order charges when ACCTAB performed its first reconciliation — money recovered from the provider.

Risk: Errors, missed income, bank charges and fraud go undetected indefinitely.

Solution: Monthly reconciliations, no exceptions. This is standard in every ACCTAB bookkeeping engagement.

5. Incorrect VAT treatment

Problem: Charging VAT while unregistered, failing to register after passing the threshold, or claiming input VAT without valid invoices.

Risk: BURS assessments, penalties and interest — VAT errors are among the most expensive mistakes on this list.

Solution: Monitor turnover against the registration threshold and let professionals prepare each return. See our Botswana Business Tax Guide for the full VAT picture.

6. Ignoring debtors until they are ancient

Problem: Invoices issued, then forgotten. No statements, no follow-up.

Risk: Cash flow starves while "profit" sits uncollected in other people's bank accounts. Old debts become uncollectable.

Solution: An aged debtors report reviewed monthly, statements sent automatically, and a firm collection routine. ACCTAB's management reports show exactly who owes you and for how long.

7. No stock records

Problem: Stock bought, sold, damaged and taken for personal use — none of it recorded.

Risk: You cannot see theft or shrinkage, your margins are unknown, and your financial statements misstate both stock and profit.

Solution: Periodic stock counts reconciled to purchases and sales. Even a simple monthly count transforms visibility.

8. Undocumented loans between owner and company

Problem: The owner injects P50,000 one month and takes P30,000 the next, with no paperwork.

Risk: Tax complications, shareholder disputes, and balance sheets no bank will trust.

Solution: Simple loan agreements and a proper loan account maintained in the books — ACCTAB handles this as part of monthly accounting.

9. Cash sales kept off the books

Problem: Card sales recorded, cash sales pocketed.

Risk: Beyond being unlawful, it destroys the business's own value: understated revenue means declined loans, weak valuations if you ever sell, and serious BURS exposure if detected.

Solution: Record everything. A business with honest books is worth more — to banks, buyers and you.

10. Confusing profit with cash

Problem: "The income statement says we made P200,000 — where is it?"

Risk: Owners draw out "profit" that is actually sitting in unpaid invoices and stock, then cannot pay salaries or VAT when due.

Solution: Review the cash flow statement alongside the income statement monthly. ACCTAB's reports explain the difference in plain language.

11. Only doing accounts once a year

Problem: A shoebox of documents lands on an accountant's desk every March.

Risk: Twelve months of undetected problems, rushed tax filings, missed deductions, and zero management information all year.

Solution: Monthly accounting. It usually costs little more than a year-end scramble and delivers infinitely more value.

12. DIY tax returns

Problem: Owners self-preparing corporate tax, VAT and PAYE filings without knowing the rules.

Risk: Overpaying tax through missed deductions — or underpaying and collecting penalties plus interest. Both are expensive.

Solution: Professional preparation. ACCTAB's tax services routinely save clients more than the fee costs.

13. No fixed asset register

Problem: Vehicles, equipment and tools bought over the years — nobody knows what exists, where it is, or what it is worth.

Risk: Missed capital allowances (real tax money), unnoticed theft, and misstated financial statements.

Solution: A maintained asset register with depreciation schedules — standard practice in ACCTAB engagements.

14. Trusting unqualified help

Problem: A relative "who is good with numbers" runs the books with no training or oversight.

Risk: Errors compound silently for years; in the worst cases, unsupervised bookkeepers commit fraud.

Solution: Use a regulated professional. ACCTAB is BICA-registered, which means professional standards, ethics and accountability stand behind every number.

15. No backups of financial records

Problem: Books live on one laptop or in one folder of paper.

Risk: Theft, fire or hardware failure erases years of records that BURS still expects you to produce.

Solution: Cloud accounting with automatic backups plus digital copies of documents. ACCTAB migrates clients to secure cloud systems as standard.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the most common accounting mistake in Botswana SMEs?

Mixing personal and business money. It distorts profits, complicates tax and hides the business's true performance. A separate account and defined drawings fix it.

Can BURS penalise me for poor record keeping?

Yes. Without supporting records, BURS can disallow expenses and raise estimated assessments with penalties and interest. Keep supporting documents for several years.

How do I fix accounting records that are already a mess?

Start from bank statements — the factual backbone. ACCTAB reconstructs books from bank data, invoices and receipts, then corrects any affected tax filings. The sooner, the cheaper.

Is a spreadsheet good enough?

Only for the tiniest operations. Spreadsheets have no audit trail and cannot handle VAT properly. Cloud accounting software is inexpensive and far more reliable.

How often should bank reconciliations be done?

Monthly at minimum — it is the single best control against error and fraud, and standard in every ACCTAB engagement.

What happens if I claimed expenses without receipts?

BURS can disallow them, increasing your taxable income plus penalties. Start capturing every receipt digitally now; ACCTAB can advise on handling past claims.

Should I register for VAT voluntarily?

It can make sense if your customers are VAT-registered businesses, but it adds filing obligations. Get advice on your specific numbers first.

Can ACCTAB review my books without taking over completely?

Yes — many clients start with a standalone books review and a prioritised fix list before deciding on monthly services.

How much does fixing a backlog cost?

It depends on the months outstanding and the state of documents. ACCTAB quotes a fixed fee after a free assessment — no surprises.

What records must I keep for BURS?

Sales and purchase invoices, receipts, bank statements, payroll records, asset registers, VAT records and contracts — retained safely for several years. Digital copies are acceptable.

My business is small — do these mistakes really matter?

Small businesses feel them hardest: one penalty or one season of hidden losses can erase thin margins. Good habits cost little; bad ones compound.
