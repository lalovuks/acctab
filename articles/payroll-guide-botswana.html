PAYE, deductions, leave, benefits and compliance — everything a Botswana employer must get right every month, and how outsourcing makes it effortless.

What Payroll Includes

Proper payroll is much more than transferring salaries. A complete payroll function covers:

  • Gross pay calculation — basic salary, overtime, commissions, allowances and bonuses.
  • Statutory deductions — PAYE computed on current BURS tax tables.
  • Voluntary deductions — pension contributions, medical aid, loan repayments, advances.
  • Payslips — a clear written record for every employee, every period.
  • Remittances — paying PAYE and third parties (pension funds, medical schemes) on time.
  • Leave management — annual, sick and maternity leave tracked against Employment Act entitlements.
  • Record keeping and reporting — payroll journals for the accounts, BURS reconciliations, and reports management can actually use.

The Monthly Payroll Process, Step by Step

  1. Collect inputs — new hires, terminations, salary changes, overtime hours, leave taken, advances.
  2. Process the run — compute gross pay, PAYE and deductions for each employee.
  3. Review and approve — a second pair of eyes checks the run against last month and flags anomalies.
  4. Pay employees — batch payments released on the agreed pay date, every time.
  5. Distribute payslips — securely, to each employee.
  6. Remit PAYE to BURS — generally by the 15th of the following month.
  7. Post to the books — payroll journals feed your accounting records so financial statements stay accurate.
ACCTAB Expert Tip

Set a monthly "payroll cut-off" date (for example the 20th) after which changes roll to the next month. Half of all payroll errors we correct come from last-minute, undocumented changes made on pay day itself.

PAYE in Botswana

Under Botswana's PAYE system, the employer — not the employee — is responsible for deducting the right income tax from every salary and remitting it to BURS. Resident individuals enjoy a tax-free band on the first portion of annual income, with progressive rates rising to the top marginal rate above the highest threshold. Rates and bands are set by BURS and can change with the national budget, which is exactly why payroll should be run on current tables, not last year's memory.

Key employer obligations:

  • Register as an employer with BURS before your first payroll run.
  • Deduct PAYE according to the current tax tables each period.
  • Remit deductions to BURS on time — generally by the 15th of the following month.
  • File the annual reconciliation and issue employees their annual tax certificates.

Pensions and Severance

Botswana has no universal compulsory private-sector pension, but retirement funds regulated by NBFIRA are a common and powerful retention benefit — and where a fund exists, contributions must be deducted, remitted and accounted for precisely. Separately, the Employment Act creates severance benefit obligations for qualifying long-serving employees, a liability many businesses never account for until an employee leaves and the bill arrives. Good payroll quantifies and provides for it in advance.

Employee Benefits

Benefits common in Botswana payrolls include housing and transport allowances, medical aid contributions, staff loans and performance bonuses. Two rules keep you safe:

  • Tax them correctly. Many benefits are taxable in the employee's hands; getting this wrong understates PAYE, and the employer carries the liability.
  • Document them. Every allowance should trace to a contract or policy — informal benefits become "acquired rights" in labour disputes.

Payroll Compliance Essentials

  • Employer registered with BURS for PAYE
  • Written contracts for every employee
  • PAYE computed on current tax tables and remitted by the 15th
  • Payslips issued every pay period
  • Leave registers maintained (annual, sick, maternity)
  • Overtime calculated at Employment Act rates
  • Annual PAYE reconciliation filed with BURS
  • Payroll records retained safely for several years

Frequently Asked Questions

What is PAYE in Botswana?

PAYE (Pay As You Earn) requires employers to deduct income tax from salaries and remit it to BURS monthly. Residents enjoy a tax-free band on the first portion of annual income, with progressive rates above it. The employer carries legal responsibility for getting it right.

When must PAYE be paid to BURS?

Generally by the 15th of the month following deduction, with an annual reconciliation also required. Late remittance attracts penalties and interest.

Is a pension compulsory for private employers?

No universal private-sector requirement exists, but NBFIRA-regulated funds are a common benefit, and Employment Act severance obligations apply regardless. ACCTAB helps structure and account for both correctly.

How much annual leave are employees entitled to?

The Employment Act provides a minimum entitlement (at least 15 working days a year for most employees) plus public holidays, sick and maternity leave. Contracts may grant more; accurate leave records are a legal requirement.

How is overtime calculated in Botswana?

Generally at 1.5 times the normal hourly rate, and double for rest days and public holidays, subject to the Employment Act and the employee's contract.

What must appear on a payslip?

Gross pay, each deduction (PAYE, pension, advances) and net pay, in writing. Payslips are key evidence in labour disputes — missing ones count against the employer.

What payroll records must an employer keep?

Contracts, attendance and overtime records, leave registers, payslips, PAYE calculations and remittance proofs, and termination documents — retained for several years.

What does payroll outsourcing cost?

Typically a fixed fee per employee per month — almost always below the true internal cost once software, time and error risk are counted. ACCTAB quotes transparently after a free consultation.

Can ACCTAB run payroll for just two or three employees?

What happens if I get PAYE wrong?

Under-deduction leaves you liable for the shortfall plus penalties; over-deduction upsets staff and creates refund admin. Professional payroll removes both risks.

How is payroll confidentiality handled?

Can historical payroll problems be fixed?

Yes. We review past payroll, recompute PAYE, correct BURS submissions and regularise records. Voluntary correction is always cheaper than a BURS discovery.
