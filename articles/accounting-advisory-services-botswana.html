What Are Accounting Advisory Services?

Compliance keeps you legal; advisory makes you money. Here is what accounting advisory actually covers — and when a Botswana business should reach for it.

Updated July 2026 By the ACCTAB Professional Team 11 min read

Most business owners meet accountants at compliance time: statements to prepare, returns to file. Accounting advisory is the other half of the profession — the half where the numbers stop being paperwork and start being strategy. It answers questions like: Should we restructure before taking on the investor? What will the new branch really cost? Is this acquisition worth the asking price? Why is profit up but cash down?

This guide explains what advisory covers, who needs it, and how ACCTAB's advisory process works for businesses across Botswana.

Who Needs Accounting Advisory?

Advisory is not a big-company luxury. It is triggered by moments, not size:

  • A business growing faster than its systems
  • An owner preparing to take on investors, partners or debt
  • A company buying, selling or merging
  • A profitable business that is always short of cash
  • A family business planning succession
  • Any business whose financial reports no longer answer its real questions

If several of those sound familiar, see our companion piece: 10 Signs Your Business Needs an Accounting Advisor.

Financial Reporting Advisory

As businesses grow, transactions outgrow simple bookkeeping: leases that must be capitalised, revenue earned across periods, foreign currency purchases, assets that need revaluation. Reporting advisory ensures your financial statements treat these correctly under IFRS or IFRS for SMEs — so banks and investors read statements they can trust, and year-end holds no nasty surprises.

Business Restructuring

Many Botswana businesses grow organically into awkward shapes: one company doing five unrelated things, assets in personal names, informal loans everywhere. Restructuring advisory untangles this — separating risk (so a claim against one operation cannot sink the others), cleaning up shareholding and intercompany balances, and organising the group for tax efficiency and succession. ACCTAB models the options with numbers, then manages the accounting and company secretarial mechanics of the change.

Growth Planning

Growth consumes cash before it returns it — the new branch pays rent months before it breaks even; the big contract demands stock and wages long before the customer pays. Growth advisory turns plans into arithmetic: break-even analysis, working capital requirements, pricing models and funding strategy. The goal is simple: grow on purpose, with a plan the numbers support.

ACCTAB Expert Tip

Before signing any major lease, contract or loan, have the numbers modelled independently. An afternoon of advisory before a commitment is worth more than a year of accounting after a bad one.

Business Acquisitions

Buying a business — or buying out a partner — is the largest cheque most owners ever write. Financial due diligence verifies what you are actually buying: are the profits real, what debts and obligations come with it, which customers actually generate the margin, and what happens to the numbers when the current owner walks away. ACCTAB investigates, values and advises on structure before you commit.

Financial Controls

Controls are the quiet infrastructure that protects money: payment approvals, bank reconciliations, segregation of duties, cash handling rules. Advisory designs controls proportionate to your business — tight enough to stop fraud and error, light enough not to slow you down. This pairs naturally with the fraud-detection value of clean books.

Complex Compliance

Some compliance moments are too consequential for routine treatment: BURS disputes and voluntary disclosures, changing accounting frameworks, regulatory registrations, or preparing the business for its first audit. Advisory brings senior attention to the situations where mistakes are most expensive.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between accounting and advisory?

Accounting records and reports what happened; advisory uses those numbers to shape what happens next. Compliance looks backward; advisory looks forward.

Is advisory only for large companies?

No — SMEs often benefit most, because single decisions have proportionally bigger impact. ACCTAB scales advisory to SME budgets, often as short focused engagements.

When would I need IFRS guidance?

When transactions get complicated: leases, multi-element revenue, foreign currency, business combinations, or first compliant statements for a bank or investor.

What does restructuring advisory involve?

Reviewing entities, shareholding, debt and intercompany balances, then reorganising for tax efficiency, risk separation or succession — with ACCTAB managing the mechanics.

Can ACCTAB help me evaluate buying another business?

Yes — financial due diligence verifies earnings, debts and cash flows, surfaces hidden liabilities, and informs price and structure before you commit.

What are financial controls?

The rules and separations that protect money: payment approvals, reconciliations, segregation of duties, cash handling. ACCTAB designs them proportionate to your size.

How does an engagement start?

A free consultation, then a fixed-fee scope with clear deliverables — cost and outcome known before you commit.

Monthly advisory or one-off projects?

Both work. Many clients run one-off projects plus a quarterly review; fast-growing businesses prefer ongoing advisory alongside monthly accounting.

Will advisory help me get funding?

Directly — advisory produces the business cases, forecasts and models that banks, CEDA and investors require, and prepares you for their questions.

What is growth planning in accounting terms?

Turning ambition into numbers: break-even, working capital, pricing and cash runway. Growth without this arithmetic is how profitable businesses die.

Is advisory confidential?

Completely — BICA professional confidentiality covers even the most sensitive matters: restructures, disputes, acquisitions.

How much does advisory cost?

Fixed fees quoted after a free consultation. Because it prevents expensive mistakes or unlocks funding, advisory is among the highest-return services a business can buy.
